Reversible Reactions

What is a Reversible Reaction?

A reversible reaction is one that can proceed in both the forward and reverse directions. It is shown with a double arrow (в‡Њ):

aA + bB в‡Њ cC + dD

The forward reaction converts reactants (A, B) to products (C, D). The reverse reaction converts products back to reactants. Not all reactions are reversible вЂ” irreversible reactions go essentially to completion (single arrow в†’).

15.2

Dynamic Equilibrium

Definition вЂ” Dynamic Equilibrium A state reached in a closed system when the rate of the forward reaction equals the rate of the reverse reaction. The concentrations of all species remain constant вЂ” but both reactions continue simultaneously at the same rate. The system appears static but is actually in constant motion at the molecular level.

Key Features of Dynamic Equilibrium

  • Closed system required вЂ” no products or reactants can escape
  • Constant macroscopic properties вЂ” concentrations, colour, pressure, and density do not change
  • Dynamic at the molecular level вЂ” forward and reverse reactions both ongoing; rates are equal (not zero)
  • Can be reached from either direction вЂ” same equilibrium state is reached whether you start with reactants or products
  • Temperature dependent вЂ” the equilibrium position changes with temperature; a new equilibrium is established
15.3

Le ChГўtelier's Principle

Le ChГўtelier's Principle (1888) "If a system at equilibrium is subjected to a change (in concentration, pressure, or temperature), the system will shift its equilibrium position to partially oppose and minimise the effect of that change."

Why "Partially Oppose"?

The system never fully neutralises the disturbance вЂ” it only partially counteracts it. For example, if you add more reactant, the equilibrium shifts forward to use some of it up вЂ” but not all. A new equilibrium is established with higher concentrations of both reactants and products than the original equilibrium, but with a higher proportion of products.

A catalyst speeds up both the forward and reverse reactions equally by providing an alternative pathway with lower activation energy. Therefore:

  • The equilibrium position is unchanged (same concentrations at equilibrium)
  • Kc is unchanged

At dynamic equilibrium, which statement is correct?

Dynamic equilibrium: forward rate = reverse rate (both ongoing simultaneously). Concentrations remain constant (not zero вЂ” both reactants and products are present). "Dynamic" = both reactions happening; "equilibrium" = net change = zero. Neither forward nor reverse has stopped.
